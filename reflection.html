The Theory Behind the Engine

When the Medium Forgets: A Poetics of Digital Ephemerality

The Core Idea

This installation explores a radical reversal of our usual understanding of communication technology. We typically celebrate tools that preserve like writing, printing, photography, cloud storage. But what about technologies designed to forget?

"Forgetting is not accidental or unfortunate but instead a designed, necessary, and culturally meaningful aspect of the history of communication."

The Ephemeral Engine makes this theory experiential. It's not a website about forgetting, it's a system that performs forgetting.

Historical Precedents

Sand Writing

Messages drawn in sand, meant to be washed away by tide or wind. Ephemeral by design.

Burned Letters

Correspondence destroyed after reading to protect privacy or prevent evidence.

Oral Tradition

Stories that mutate with each telling, never fixed in permanent form.

24-hour Stories

Modern digital equivalents: Snapchat and Instagram Stories thate are designed to disappear.

The Psychology of Forgetting

Why would we design systems to forget?

  • Protection: Some memories are dangerous to keep
  • Healing: Letting go as part of emotional processing
  • Privacy: Resistance against surveillance culture
  • Intimacy: Conversations that exist only in the moment
  • Reinvention: Space to become someone new

Design Principles of the Engine

1. Intentionality

Forgetting here is not accidental, it's a deliberate ritual. Users choose what to release and how.

2. Poetics

The process is aestheticized: erosion, burning, mutation. Forgetting becomes beautiful.

3. Ethics of Absence

The archive records only traces, not content. We remember that we forgot, not what we forgot.

4. Temporal Design

The system exists in time, processes have duration, creating space for reflection.

Guiding Questions

This project invites consideration of:

What happens to communication when memory is not guaranteed?
In an age of total recall, what is the value of forgetting?
Can forgetting be understood as liberation rather than loss?
What memories do we need to lose to move forward?

Experience the Theory

The ideas above are not just academic, they're built into the fabric of this installation. The best way to understand them is to experience them.
